Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know ShieldVault is working?

Click the ShieldVault icon in your browser toolbar. If protection is active, you'll see "Proofs of Prevention" showing any blocked secrets. You can also test by pasting a fake API key pattern like sk-test1234567890abcdefghij into a supported chat.

Which sites does ShieldVault protect?

ShieldVault currently protects: ChatGPT (chat.openai.com, chatgpt.com), Claude (claude.ai), Gemini (gemini.google.com), Perplexity (perplexity.ai), and Microsoft Copilot (copilot.microsoft.com).

Does ShieldVault store my secrets?

No. ShieldVault never stores, logs, or transmits the actual content of detected secrets. It only keeps session-only metadata (type of secret detected, domain, timestamp) that disappears when you close your browser.

Why was my text blocked when it wasn't a secret?

ShieldVault uses pattern matching that may occasionally flag text that resembles API key formats. This is intentional—we err on the side of caution. If you're confident the text is safe, you can retype it character by character to bypass detection.

Can I disable ShieldVault temporarily?

Yes. Click the extension icon in Chrome, then go to "Manage Extensions" and toggle ShieldVault off. Remember to turn it back on when you're done.

ShieldVault isn't blocking secrets on a site

Make sure you're on one of the supported sites listed above. If you are and it's still not working, try refreshing the page or reinstalling the extension. If the issue persists, contact us.
